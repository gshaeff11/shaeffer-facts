Rocks

  • Tiger‚Äôs Eye: A golden-brown metamorphic rock with a silky luster. Hardness: 6-7 Mohs. Found in South Africa, Australia, and India.
  • Geodes: Hollow rock formations with crystal interiors. Hardness: Varies (quartz 7, calcite 3). Found in volcanic regions and deserts.
  • Snowflake Obsidian: A black volcanic glass with white patterns. Hardness: 5-5.5 Mohs. Found in the U.S., Mexico, and Argentina.
  • Calcite: A carbonate mineral with many colors. Hardness: 3 Mohs. Found worldwide in limestone caves and marine deposits.
  • Rose Quartz: A pink variety of quartz known for its beauty. Hardness: 7 Mohs. Found in Brazil, Madagascar, and the U.S.
  • Desert Rose: A crystal formation of gypsum or barite shaped like a rose. Hardness: 1.5-3.5 Mohs. Found in deserts like the Sahara and southwestern U.S.
  • Pumice: A lightweight volcanic rock full of gas bubbles. Hardness: 6 Mohs. Found in volcanic regions like Italy and the U.S.
  • Agate: A colorful, banded variety of quartz. Hardness: 6.5-7 Mohs. Found in Brazil, India, and the U.S.
  • Fluorite: A brightly colored mineral that fluoresces under UV light. Hardness: 4 Mohs. Found in China, Mexico, and South Africa.
  • Flint: A hard, sedimentary cryptocrystalline quartz used for tools. Hardness: 7 Mohs. Found in the U.K., U.S., and France.
